<section id="news">
<h2>News</h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://arstechnica.com/tech-policy/2026/02/fbi-stymied-by-apples-lockdown-mode-after-seizing-journalists-iphone/" target="_blank">FBI stymied by Apple’s Lockdown Mode after seizing journalist’s iPhone</a> - "Because the iPhone was in Lockdown mode, CART could not extract that device." (<a href="https://storage.courtlistener.com/recap/gov.uscourts.vaed.588772/gov.uscourts.vaed.588772.35.0_1.pdf" target="_blank">[PDF] court filing</a> ). However, the use of biometrics allowed the FBI into her work computer, "when she applied her index finger to the fingerprint reader, the laptop unlocked." While convenient, biometrics should not be used if you're a potential target of device seizure. It would be interesting if there was a "distress finger" feature that could be used to prevent any future biometrics from working. Map this to your index finger but actually unlock with your middle or ring finger.</li>
<li><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OheUzrXsKrY" target="_blank">[YouTube] Search Party from Ring | Be A Hero In Your Neighborhood</a> - This ran during the Super Bowl in the US (~8M USD cost). I wonder if the marketing team had a big debate over using puppies vs children and the pros and cons of each to sell an omnipresent surveillance dystopia. Recall that <a href="https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/news/press-releases/2023/05/ftc-says-ring-employees-illegally-surveilled-customers-failed-stop-hackers-taking-control-users" target="_blank">Ring Employees Illegally Surveilled Customers</a> and <a href="https://www.flocksafety.com/blog/flock-safety-and-ring-partner-to-help-neighborhoods-work-together-for-safer-communities" target="_blank">Ring and Flock Safety have partnered</a> and that Ring cameras are part of <a href="https://ring.com/support/articles/b4lyi/Amazon-Sidewalk-Compatible-Devices" target="_blank">Amazon Sidewalk</a> (the mesh network Amazon built to keep cameras online).</li>
<li><a href="https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cz7y2ddgl23o" target="_blank">Iran's internet is returning - but not for everyone</a> - "Looking at web traffic data, the patterns suggest a full restoration of internet access in Iran may never occur." In 2026 the internet is a utility like water and power. I suspect a regime denying it's people access will result in regime change.</li>
<li><a href="https://www.outflank.nl/blog/2026/01/21/red-macros-factory-is-joining-ost-and-so-am-i/" target="_blank">Red Macros Factory Is Joining OST (And So Am I!)</a> - Congrats <a href="https://x.com/mariuszbit" target="_blank">@mariuszbit</a>! Forta is assembling a super team.</li>
<li><a href="https://www.forbes.com/sites/thomasbrewster/2026/01/22/microsoft-gave-fbi-keys-to-unlock-bitlocker-encrypted-data/" target="_blank">Microsoft Gave FBI Keys To Unlock Encrypted Data, Exposing Major Privacy Flaw</a> - If you sign in with a Microsoft account, you sync your Bitlocker keys to Microsoft. Senator Ron Wyden said in a statement to Forbes that it is “simply irresponsible for tech companies to ship products in a way that allows them to secretly turn over users' encryption keys.”</li>

<section id="techniques-and-write-ups">
<h2>Techniques and Write-ups</h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://www.rapid7.com/blog/post/tr-chrysalis-backdoor-dive-into-lotus-blossoms-toolkit/" target="_blank">The Chrysalis Backdoor: A Deep Dive into Lotus Blossom’s toolkit</a> - China (allegedly) hacked the Notepad++ update servers and selectively targeted users with a malicious update.</li>
<li><a href="https://spaceraccoon.dev/discovering-negative-days-llm-workflows/" target="_blank">Discovering Negative-Days with LLM Workflows</a> - The big threat actors are already doing this for both open and closed source projects. At what point does your threat intel team have to be come a vulnerability development shop just to keep up?</li>
<li><a href="https://www.elastic.co/security-labs/automating-goad-and-live-malware-labs" target="_blank">Automating GOAD and Live Malware Labs</a> - <a href="https://ludus.cloud" target="_blank">Ludus</a> being used on the official Elastic Labs blog, really cool to see.</li>
<li><a href="https://starlabs.sg/blog/2026/01-pickling-the-mailbox-a-deep-dive-into-cve-2025-20393/" target="_blank">Pickling the Mailbox: A Deep Dive into CVE-2025-20393</a> - Python 2.6 struct.pack with format ‘B’ silently truncates overflows instead of throwing an error like Python 3 does. This allows an authentication bypass and a CVSS score of 10.0.</li>
<li><a href="https://labs.watchtowr.com/someone-knows-bash-far-too-well-and-we-love-it-ivanti-epmm-pre-auth-rces-cve-2026-1281-cve-2026-1340/" target="_blank">Someone Knows Bash Far Too Well, And We Love It (Ivanti EPMM Pre-Auth RCEs CVE-2026-1281 &amp; CVE-2026-1340)</a> - "Any bash script &gt; 50 lines should be written in something else." - Me, every time I write a long bash script. "The PoC request appeared in a dream" and not your honeypot? Sure 😉.</li>
<li><a href="https://sean.heelan.io/2026/01/18/on-the-coming-industrialisation-of-exploit-generation-with-llms/" target="_blank">On the Coming Industrialisation of Exploit Generation with LLMs</a> - "I would encourage you to pick the most interesting exploitation related problem you can think of, spend as many tokens as you can afford on it, and write up the results. You may be surprised by how well it works." Code: <a href="https://github.com/SeanHeelan/anamnesis-release" target="_blank">anamnesis-release</a> - Automatic Exploit Generation with LLMs.</li>
<li><a href="https://research.checkpoint.com/2026/voidlink-the-cloud-native-malware-framework/" target="_blank">Unveiling Voidlink – a Stealthy, Cloud-Native Linux Malware Framework</a> - A neat looking Linux remote access tool. Surprised with that level of sophistication it calls directly back to a command and control server vs using a "third party" service to launder the data and appear legitimate (see next item).</li>
<li><a href="https://specterops.io/blog/2026/01/30/weaponizing-whitelists-an-azure-blob-storage-mythic-c2-profile/" target="_blank">Weaponizing Whitelists: An Azure Blob Storage Mythic C2 Profile</a> - "3rd party C2" is my favorite kind of C2. Don't use this with an Azure account you care about, if threat intel finds you they may nuke your account.</li>
<li><a href="https://swarm.ptsecurity.com/whos-on-the-line-exploiting-rce-in-windows-telephony-service/" target="_blank">Who’s on the Line? Exploiting RCE in Windows Telephony Service</a> - Legacy, obscure systems almost always house some vulnerabilities. This vulnerability requires the the Telephony Application Programming Interface (TAPI), to be in server mode, which you will likely never see.</li>
<li><a href="https://www.synacktiv.com/en/publications/on-the-clock-escaping-vmware-workstation-at-pwn2own-berlin-2025" target="_blank">On the Clock: Escaping VMware Workstation at Pwn2own Berlin 2025</a> - Some advanced heap exploitation along with a timing-channel leak to reduce the randominies of Windows 11 heaps and ensure a good exploit make this a really interesting read.</li>
<li><a href="https://aff-wg.org/2026/02/02/the-islands-of-invariance/" target="_blank">The Islands of Invariance</a> - Mudge added a Yara rule generator to Crystal Palace, has he gone to the blue side!? Not quite, but the robust testing of Yara rules in this post puts him ahead of many blue teams. He also released a major overhaul to Crystal Palace in the <a href="https://aff-wg.org/2026/01/13/keeping-bin2bin-out-of-the-bin/" target="_blank">bin2bin</a> post that include the <cite>+regdance</cite>, <cite>+blockparty</cite>, and <cite>+shatter</cite> options as well as support for the <cite>-O1</cite> optimization level in MinGW.</li>
<li><a href="https://blog.amberwolf.com/blog/2026/january/advisory---check-point-harmony-local-privilege-escalation-cve-2025-9142/" target="_blank">Advisory - Check Point Harmony Local Privilege Escalation (CVE-2025-9142)</a> - AmberWolf drops another VPN client vulnerability. Finding the unregistered but allowed domain must have been a rush!</li>
<li><a href="https://code-white.com/blog/2026-01-nsm-rce/" target="_blank">Unauthenticated RCE in NetSupport Manager - A Technical Deep Dive</a> - The heap graphics in this write up were excellent. <a href="https://www.netsupportmanager.com/" target="_blank">NetSupport Manager</a>  is a "remote monitoring and management solution" (remote desktop replacement) so getting access to the controller means access to all the managed machines.</li>
<li><a href="https://connormcgarr.github.io/securitytrace-etw-ppl/" target="_blank">Windows Internals: Check Your Privilege - The Curious Case of ETW's SecurityTrace Flag</a> - Connor does what he does best and dives deep on Event Tracing for Windows (ETW) and finds an undocumented way to consume events from ETW providers which require Antimalware-PPL, like the valuable Microsoft-Windows-Threat-Intelligence, without running as Antimalware-PPL, having a kernel driver, or patching the kernel via WinDBG. He was nice enough to drop <a href="https://github.com/preludeorg/ThreatIntelligenceConsumer" target="_blank">ThreatIntelligenceConsumer</a> - Demonstrates consuming from a SecurityTrace ETW session by consuming from the Threat-Intelligence ETW provider without a driver or PPL privilege.</li>
<li><a href="https://x-c3ll.github.io/posts/Rant-Red-Team/" target="_blank">The State of Art in Red Team is whatever you want to believe</a> - Red Teams are islands, and you have no idea what the other top red teams are doing because there is no incentive to share the truely useful stuff.</li>
<li><a href="https://mrbruh.com/firewreckv2/" target="_blank">1.4 Billion user records exposed by insecure Firebase instances in top Android apps</a> - Firebase has always been a massive foot-gun. The easy solution gets used, and most apps never notice that any user can read/write their entire database.</li>
<li><a href="https://specterops.io/blog/2026/01/13/introducing-configmanbearpig-a-bloodhound-opengraph-collector-for-sccm/" target="_blank">Introducing ConfigManBearPig, a BloodHound OpenGraph Collector for SCCM</a> - SCCM in bloodhound is awesome. No more hoping the organization used hostnames that indicate SCCM or digging through LDAP data to find it! This research led to two CVEs: <a href="https://specterops.io/blog/2026/01/15/mssql-and-sccm-elevation-of-privilege-vulnerabilities/" target="_blank">MSSQL and SCCM Elevation of Privilege Vulnerabilities</a>.</li>
<li><a href="https://jyn.dev/remotely-unlocking-an-encrypted-hard-disk/" target="_blank">remotely unlocking an encrypted hard disk</a> - You've maybe seen <a href="https://github.com/r-pufky/wireguard-initramfs" target="_blank">wireguard-initramfs</a> but this post uses tailscale.</li>
<li><a href="https://specterops.io/blog/2026/01/21/task-failed-successfully-microsofts-immediate-retirement-of-mdt/" target="_blank">Task Failed Successfully – Microsoft’s “Immediate” Retirement of MDT</a> - Retiring a product instead of fixing vulnerabilities is a choice. Another <a href="https://ludus.cloud" target="_blank">Ludus</a> powered finding.</li>
</ul>
